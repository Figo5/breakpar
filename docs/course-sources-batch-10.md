## Notes

**Card verified nine-by-nine.** OUT 3,870 / par 36 and IN 3,950 / par 36 both reconcile against the published subtotals, so the routing is internally consistent, not a mis-transcription.

**Stroke-index parity.** The published index row already satisfied the repo's one-parity-per-nine convention (front all odd, back all even) with no adjustment. No placeholder/sequential index row.

**The 2nd is genuinely a 275-yard par 3.** This looked like bad source data — it would be among the longest par 3s anywhere — so it was checked before use rather than accepted. Course reviews independently describe the 2nd as "a monster and almost certainly the most difficult par 3 at Congressional," an uphill long one-shotter, and the yardage row reconciles to the published OUT subtotal. Kept as published.

**Known tee-dependent discrepancy on the 10th.** Break Par carries 170 yards from the Back card. Post-restoration coverage describes the 10th as a downhill par 3 of ~218 yards. Both refer to the same hole from different tees; the Back-card figure was kept for internal consistency with the rest of the routing. Flagged here rather than silently reconciled. Note yardage is display-only in Break Par — the engine derives difficulty from par, stroke index and course conditions, so this affects presentation only.

**This card is post-restoration.** Andrew Green's rebuild opened in 2021 and lengthened the course substantially; it does not match the 2011 U.S. Open setup (which played 7,574 to a par of 71, and whose 1st was 402 yards against 445 here).

**Signature holes** follow documented history: the 10th is the Rees Jones par 3 built when the club permanently reversed the old 18th ahead of the 2011 U.S. Open, and is widely called the Blue's most famous hole; the 18th is the original par-4 17th, now the permanent finisher, where Rory McIlroy completed his record 16-under win in 2011.
